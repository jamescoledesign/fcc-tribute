Bourdain

About

Anthony Michael Bourdain was an American celebrity chef, author, and travel documentarian who starred in programs focusing on the exploration of international culture, cuisine, and the human condition.

Bourdain sits down for lunch with Hausa people in Lagos, Nigeria, in 2017.
Beginnings

Anthony Bourdain was born June 25, 1956, in New York City and spent most of his childhood in Leonia, New Jersey. He graduated from the Dwight-Englewood School in 1973, then enrolled at Vassar College, but dropped out after two years. He worked in seafood restaurants in Provincetown, Massachusetts, while attending Vassar, which inspired his decision to pursue cooking as a career. Bourdain attended The Culinary Institute of America, graduating in 1978.

Career

Bourdain went on to run restaurant kitchens in New York Cityâ€”including the Supper Club, One Fifth Avenue, and Sullivan's.In 1998, Bourdain became executive chef at Brasserie Les Halles. Based in Manhattan, which had additional restaurants in Miami, Washington, D.C., and Tokyo. Bourdain remained executive chef there for many years.

Bourdain published Kitchen Confidential: Adventures in the Culinary Underbelly in 2000, and became a New York Times bestseller. A sequel to the book, Medium Raw: A Bloody Valentine to the World of Food and the People Who Cook, was published in 2010. He wrote a number of other books throuought his life, including two more bestselling nonfiction books.

The acclaim surrounding Bourdain's memoir Kitchen Confidential led to an offer by the Food Network for him to host his own food and world-travel show, A Cook's Tour, which premiered in January 2002. It ran for 35 episodes, through 2003.

From 2005 to 2012, he hosted a similar television series, Anthony Bourdain: No Reservations, on the Travel Channel. The special episode Anthony Bourdain in Beirut that aired between Seasons 2 and 3 was nominated for an Emmy Award for Outstanding Informational Programming in 2007. In 2009 and 2011 the series won the Emmy for "Outstanding Cinematography For Nonfiction Programming".

From 2013 to 2018, he went on to host Anthony Bourdain: Parts Unknown for CNN. The program focuses on other cuisines, cultures and politics. The show was filmed and is set in places as diverse as Libya, Tokyo, the Punjab region, Jamaica, Turkey, Ethiopia, Nigeria, Far West Texas, and Armenia.
